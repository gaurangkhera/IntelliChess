How to think.

Chess is an abstract strategy game that involves no hidden information and no elements of chance. You must think carefully before every move.

1

Capture.

Make sure to take out as many enemy pieces as possible, without taking huge risks.

2

Protect.

Make sure your important pieces such as the Queen, the King, the Bishop, the Knight and the Rook are protected.

3

Have fun.

The most important thing in every game, is to have fun. It is important to not get burnt out of playing chess.

Chess Terms

Checkmate

Checkmate is the condition in chess when a player's king is in a position to be captured (in "check") and there is no legal move to remove the threat. It is the ultimate goal of the game.

Stalemate

Stalemate is a situation where the player whose turn it is to move has no legal moves but is not in check. In this case, the game ends in a draw, and neither player wins.

Promotion

Promotion occurs when a pawn reaches the opposite end of the chessboard, also known as the eighth rank. The pawn can be promoted to any other piece of the player's choice, except another king.

Fork

A fork is a tactic where a single piece attacks two or more opponent's pieces simultaneously. The attacking piece puts pressure on the opponent, forcing them to make a difficult choice.

Pin

A pin is a situation where a piece is under attack and cannot move without exposing a more valuable piece behind it to capture. The pinned piece is temporarily immobilized.

Chess Tips and Tricks

Control the Center

Try to place your pieces in the central squares of the board (e4, e5, d4, d5) to exert influence and create more options for your moves.

Develop Your Pieces

Move your knights and bishops out early in the game to control more squares and prepare for castling.

Protect Your King

Castle early to safeguard your king and connect your rooks. It improves the king's safety and activates the rook.

Think Ahead

Anticipate your opponent's moves and plan your own strategy. Consider possible threats and opportunities in each move.

Take Your Time

Don't rush your moves. Analyze the position, evaluate different options, and choose the best move based on the current situation.

Use Tactics

Learn basic tactics like forks, pins, skewers, and discovered attacks. Look for opportunities to execute these tactical maneuvers.

Trade Wisely

Evaluate the value of the pieces being exchanged. Consider if a trade benefits your position or weakens your opponent's.

Centralize Your Pieces

Aim to have your pieces centralized, where they have maximum influence and are ready for coordinated attacks or defense.

Control Open Files

Place your rooks on open files (columns without pawns) to control them and apply pressure on your opponent's position.

Never Give Up

Even if you're in a difficult position, keep fighting and look for counterplay. A single blunder from your opponent can turn the game around.
